Reflection

Taking these photos for Homework 3 was a challenging yet rewarding experience that taught me a lot about depth of field and creative control in photography. I respect people who take pictures much more now because getting the effect I wanted was harder than expected. When capturing the shallow and deep depth of field images, I had to carefully consider my aperture settings and the distance between the subject and background. For the shallow depth of field shot, I used a wide aperture to isolate the subject and create a blurred background, emphasizing the focal point and adding a professional feel. For the deep depth of field photo, I had to use a smaller aperture, which was challenging after learning how to set up the shallow shot. Editing added another layer of complexity. Photoshop was new to me, and I initially felt lost, but following tutorials helped me apply blur and adjustment tools effectively. Overall, this assignment deepened my understanding of both technical and artistic aspects of photography. It also made me more aware of storytelling through visual choices, from framing and focus to editing and tonal adjustments. This experience strengthened my skills and gave me confidence in experimenting creatively while applying technical knowledge.
